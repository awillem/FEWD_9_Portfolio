About Me

I graduated from high school in 1999. Just after that, I picked up my first book on HTML, HTML for Dummies. I loved what I learned, but had no other education in web development and no opportunities to do anything with my skills. I followed other avenues, and eventually received my Associates Degree in Accounting which I now use working in the payroll industry.

Over the years, my interest in web development was always there, so I occassionally picked up new books and learned a little more. In 2015, I found Treehouse and started taking some of their courses. I also took a few other courses through other online platforms, but in the summer of 2018, I came back to Treehouse and jumped in with both feet, enrolling in their Full Stack Javascript Techdegree

The Treehouse Techdegree really helped me focus my learning. There was a great support system in their use of Slack, where they have a community of other students and moderators where great discussion happens. For me, the best part of this techdegree were the unit projects. After completing a handful of courses, there is a project that students complete on their own. Being able to complete these projects really helped me understand what could be accomplished while coding.

When I completed the techdegree in November of 2018, I was asked to stay on as a moderator. Since then, I have helped other students with their javascript questions and reviewed their projects. Most recently, I began going through the Front End Web Development Techdegree, which I will be completing soon.
